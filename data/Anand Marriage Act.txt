
### **1. Overview and Purpose**
* [cite_start]**Objective:** The primary purpose of the Act is to remove doubts regarding the validity of the marriage ceremony common among Sikhs known as "Anand"[cite: 889].
* [cite_start]**Date of Enactment:** The Act was enacted on October 22, 1909[cite: 888].
* [cite_start]**Extent:** The Act extends to the whole of India[cite: 893].

### **2. Validity of Marriages**
* [cite_start]**Legal Recognition:** All marriages duly solemnized according to the Sikh marriage ceremony called **Anand** (also known as **Anand Karaj**) are deemed good and valid in law[cite: 894].
* [cite_start]**Retrospective Effect:** This validity applies to marriages solemnized both before and after the Act came into effect[cite: 894].
* [cite_start]**Other Ceremonies:** The Act does not affect the validity of marriages solemnized according to other marriage ceremonies customary among Sikhs[cite: 898].

### **3. Exceptions and Restrictions**
The Act does **not** apply to or validate the following:
* [cite_start]**Non-Sikhs:** Marriages between persons who do not profess the Sikh religion[cite: 896].
* [cite_start]**Annulled Marriages:** Any marriage that has been judicially declared null and void[cite: 897].
* [cite_start]**Prohibited Degrees:** Marriages between persons related by consanguinity or affinity (blood or marriage relationships) that would be considered illegal under the customary law of the Sikhs[cite: 899].

### **4. Registration of Marriages (Section 6)**
[cite_start]State Governments are mandated to make rules to facilitate the proof of Anand Karaj marriages[cite: 900]. Key provisions include:
* [cite_start]**Entry of Particulars:** Parties to an Anand marriage (solemnized before or after the *Anand Marriage (Amendment) Act, 2012*) may have their marriage particulars entered in a Marriage Register[cite: 900].
* [cite_start]**Authority:** The register is kept by an officer of the State Government or a local authority authorized for this purpose[cite: 901].
* [cite_start]**Evidence:** The Marriage Register is open for inspection and is admissible as evidence of the statements contained within it[cite: 902].
* [cite_start]**Exemption from Other Laws:** Parties who register their marriage under this Act are not required to register under any other law currently in force[cite: 912].
* [cite_start]**Non-Mandatory for Validity:** The validity of an Anand Marriage is **not** affected if the parties omit to make an entry in the Marriage Register[cite: 903].
