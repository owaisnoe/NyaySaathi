
### **1. Introduction**
[cite_start]The Railways Act, 1989 (Act No. 24 of 1989) was enacted to consolidate and amend the law relating to Railways in India[cite: 5994]. [cite_start]It replaced the older Indian Railways Act, 1890, to reflect the changes in the railway system, which had transitioned from private management to government control [cite: 7173-7175].

**Key Objectives:**
* Consolidate laws relating to railways.
* Provide for the efficient administration of government railways.
* Regulate carriage of passengers and goods.
* Fixation of rates and tribunal matters.
* Define liabilities, offences, and penalties.

### **2. Administration and Structure**
* [cite_start]**Railway Board:** Constituted under Section 2A, the Railway Board exercises the powers and functions of the Central Government with respect to railways [cite: 6088-6089].
* [cite_start]**Zonal Railways:** The Central Government can constitute Zonal Railways for efficient administration, headed by General Managers[cite: 6105, 6109].
* [cite_start]**Rail Land Development Authority (RLDA):** Established to develop railway land for commercial use and other purposes[cite: 6113, 6126].
* [cite_start]**Commissioners of Railway Safety:** Appointed to inspect railways, investigate accidents, and ensure safety standards are met before opening new railways [cite: 6144-6145].

### **3. Construction and Maintenance**
Railway administrations have extensive powers to execute necessary works for constructing or maintaining a railway.
* [cite_start]**Powers:** They can construct bridges, tunnels, lines, and divert rivers or roads temporarily or permanently [cite: 6161-6164].
* [cite_start]**Land Acquisition:** Special provisions exist for acquiring land for "special railway projects" to ensure timely infrastructure development[cite: 6234].
* [cite_start]**Safety:** The Central Government can issue directions to close or regulate works that endanger railway safety[cite: 6230].

### **4. Carriage of Passengers**
* [cite_start]**Timings and Fares:** Railways must display timetables and fare lists at stations[cite: 6447].
* [cite_start]**Tickets:** Passengers must be supplied with tickets containing details like fare, class, and date[cite: 6453].
* **Prohibitions:** Transfer of tickets is prohibited. [cite_start]Travelling without a proper pass or ticket is an offence[cite: 6467, 6473].
* [cite_start]**Safety:** Railways can refuse to carry persons with infectious diseases and can reserve compartments for ladies[cite: 6475, 6482].

### **5. Carriage of Goods**
* [cite_start]**Railway Receipt (RR):** Issued upon acceptance of goods, serving as prima facie evidence of weight and packages[cite: 6513]. It is a negotiable instrument.
* [cite_start]**Forwarding Note:** Consignors must execute a forwarding note detailing the goods[cite: 6505].
* [cite_start]**Liability:** Railways are responsible for loss, destruction, or damage to goods in transit, with specific exceptions like act of God, war, or negligence of the consignor[cite: 6674].
* [cite_start]**Monetary Liability:** Unless the value is declared and a percentage charge paid, the liability is limited to a prescribed amount based on weight [cite: 6730-6731].


### **6. Rates and Tribunal**
* [cite_start]**Fixation of Rates:** The Central Government has the power to fix rates for the carriage of passengers and goods[cite: 6383].
* [cite_start]**Railway Rates Tribunal:** Now replaced by the **Railway Claims Tribunal**, it handles complaints regarding unreasonable rates or other charges[cite: 6396, 6407].

### **7. Accidents and Liability**
* [cite_start]**Notice:** Railway administration must give notice of accidents resulting in loss of life or grievous hurt[cite: 6777].
* [cite_start]**Inquiry:** The Commissioner of Railway Safety conducts inquiries into serious accidents[cite: 6779].
* [cite_start]**Compensation:** The railway administration is liable to pay compensation for death or injury to passengers due to accidents or "untoward incidents" (like terrorist acts, robbery, or accidental falling)[cite: 6828, 6831]. [cite_start]This is a "no-fault" liability, meaning negligence doesn't need to be proved by the claimant[cite: 6834].

### **8. Offences and Penalties**
The Act prescribes penalties for various offences to ensure discipline and safety.
* [cite_start]**Ticketless Travel:** Punishable with imprisonment or fine, plus excess charge [cite: 6920-6924].
* [cite_start]**Alarm Chain Pulling:** Needlessly interfering with communication means is punishable[cite: 6951].
* [cite_start]**Hawking and Begging:** Prohibited without a license[cite: 6969].
* [cite_start]**Roof Traveling:** Travelling on the roof or steps is an offence[cite: 7021].
* [cite_start]**Trespass:** Entering railway premises without authority is punishable[cite: 6983].
* [cite_start]**Malicious Acts:** Wrecking a train or hurting persons is a serious offence punishable with life imprisonment or even death in extreme cases[cite: 6997, 7005].
