
### **1. Introduction and Objectives**
The Act was passed on May 8, 1955, with the primary objective of establishing the **State Bank of India (SBI)**. This was done to transfer the undertaking of the **Imperial Bank of India** to the new entity and to extend banking facilities on a large scale, particularly in rural and semi-urban areas.

### **2. Establishment and Capital**
* **Incorporation:** The State Bank of India is a body corporate with perpetual succession and a common seal.
* **Capital:** The authorized capital is set at **₹5,000 crores**, divided into shares of ₹10 each. The Central Government, in consultation with the Reserve Bank of India (RBI), can increase or reduce this capital.
* **Government Holding:** The Central Government must always hold at least **51%** of the issued equity capital.

### **3. Transfer of Undertaking**
On the "appointed day" (July 1, 1955), the entire undertaking of the **Imperial Bank of India**—including all assets, liabilities, rights, powers, and employees—was transferred to the State Bank of India. [cite_start]Shareholders of the Imperial Bank were entitled to compensation as per the First Schedule of the Act [cite: 5475-5476].

### **4. Management Structure**
* **Central Board:** The general superintendence and direction of the bank's affairs are entrusted to the **Central Board of Directors**.
* **Composition of Central Board:**
    * Chairman (appointed by Central Govt).
    * Managing Directors (up to 4, appointed by Central Govt).
    * Directors elected by shareholders.
    * Directors nominated by the Central Government (representing various interests like co-operative institutions, rural economy, etc.).
    * One director nominated by the Central Government on the recommendation of the RBI.
* **Local Boards:** Constituted at local head offices (Mumbai, Kolkata, Chennai, etc.) to advise the Central Board and perform delegated duties.

### **5. Business Functions**
The State Bank acts as an agent of the **Reserve Bank of India** at all places in India where it has a branch but the RBI does not. Its key functions include:
* Paying, receiving, collecting, and remitting money/bullion on behalf of the Government.
* Carrying on the business of banking as defined in the Banking Regulation Act, 1949.
* It can acquire the business of other banking institutions with the sanction of the Central Government.

### **6. Funds and Accounts**
* **Integration and Development Fund:** Maintained to meet losses from branches established for developmental purposes (rural/semi-urban expansion) beyond what is commercially viable.
* **Reserve Fund:** Consists of the reserve fund of the Imperial Bank transferred to SBI and further sums transferred from net profits.
* **Audit:** Affairs are audited by two or more auditors appointed by the State Bank with the previous approval of the RBI. The auditors' report must be laid before Parliament.

### **7. Miscellaneous Provisions**
* **Secrecy:** The bank observes customary banking practices regarding fidelity and secrecy. Officers must sign a declaration of fidelity.
* **Indemnity:** Directors and members of boards are indemnified against losses incurred in the discharge of their duties, except those caused by willful act or default.
* **Liquidation:** The State Bank cannot be placed in liquidation except by an order of the Central Government.
