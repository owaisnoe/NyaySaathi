The **Airports Authority of India Act, 1994** (Act No. 55 of 1994) is an Act of the Parliament of India that provides for the constitution of the **Airports Authority of India (AAI)**. It facilitates the better administration and cohesive management of airports, civil enclaves, and aeronautical communication stations across India.

This Act officially merged the undertakings of two previous bodies: the **International Airports Authority of India** and the **National Airports Authority**.

### **1. Objectives and Applicability**
The primary objective is to create a unified authority responsible for the development, finance, operation, and maintenance of airports.
* **Applicability:** The Act applies to all airports (except those under the armed forces), civil enclaves, aeronautical communication stations, and training establishments related to air transport services.
* **Private Airports:** It also applies to private airports specifically regarding air traffic services and issuing directions for security or national interest.

### **2. Constitution of the Authority**
* **Body Corporate:** The Authority is a body corporate with perpetual succession, a common seal, and the power to acquire property and contract.
* **Members:** It consists of a Chairperson, the Director General of Civil Aviation (ex officio), and 8 to 14 other members appointed by the Central Government.
* **Disqualification:** Individuals convicted of offences involving moral turpitude, undischarged insolvents, or those with financial conflicts of interest cannot be members.


### **3. Key Functions (Section 12)**
The AAI is mandated to manage airports, civil enclaves, and aeronautical communication stations efficiently. Its specific functions include:
* **Infrastructure:** Planning, developing, constructing, and maintaining runways, terminals, and navigational aids.
* **Services:** Providing air traffic service, air transport service, air safety services, and search and rescue facilities.
* **Facilities:** Establishing warehouses, cargo complexes, hotels, and arranging postal or money exchange facilities.
* **Training:** Establishing institutions for training officers and employees.
* **Consultancy:** Providing consultancy and management services in India and abroad.

### **4. Financial Provisions**
* **Fees and Rent:** The Authority can charge fees for landing, parking, air traffic services, and passenger amenities with Central Government approval.
* **Development Fees:** The Authority can levy development fees on embarking passengers to fund airport upgradation, new airport establishment, or equity investment in private airports.
* **Fund:** The Authority maintains its own fund where all receipts are credited. It acts on business principles.

### **5. Property and Contracts**
* **Vesting of Assets:** All assets, rights, debts, and liabilities of the former International Airports Authority and National Airports Authority were transferred to the AAI.
* **Leasing:** The Authority may lease airport premises to third parties for better management, provided it retains control over air traffic services and security.
* **Land Acquisition:** Land needed by the Authority is deemed needed for a public purpose and can be acquired under the Land Acquisition Act.

### **6. Eviction of Unauthorized Occupants (Chapter VA)**
The Act establishes a mechanism to evict unauthorized occupants from airport premises:
* **Eviction Officers:** The Authority appoints officers to handle eviction proceedings.
* **Process:** The officer issues a show-cause notice, conducts a hearing, and can order eviction if satisfied the occupation is unauthorized.
* **damages:** The officer can assess and require payment of damages for unauthorized use of premises.
* **Offences:** Unlawful occupation of airport premises is a punishable offence with imprisonment up to six years.

### **7. Government Control**
* **Directives:** The Authority is bound by Central Government directions on policy matters.
* **Supersession:** The Central Government can supersede the Authority for up to six months in cases of grave emergency or persistent default.
* **Divestment:** The Government can temporarily divest the Authority of the management of any airport in the public interest.
